About LSAT & LNAT

The LSAT is required for US/Canadian law schools while the LNAT is required for UK law schools like Oxford and Cambridge. Both test critical reasoning and analytical skills at a high level.

  • LSAT: 120–180 score scale
  • LNAT: MCQ section + Essay section
  • LSAT: Logical Reasoning, Analytical Reasoning, Reading Comprehension
  • LNAT: 42 MCQs in 95 mins + 40 min essay

Key Topics

Logical Reasoning — Argument Analysis
Analytical Reasoning — Logic Games
Reading Comprehension — Dense Passages
LNAT — Critical Thinking MCQs
LNAT — Essay Writing

Exam Pattern

  • LSAT: 4–5 sections · 35 mins each · ~100 questions
  • LNAT: Section A (42 MCQs, 95 mins) + Section B (Essay, 40 mins)
  • LSAT is administered digitally via ProctorU
  • LNAT score sent directly to universities

Prep Tips

  • Logic Games are learnable — practice daily
  • Time management is critical for LSAT
  • For LNAT essay, practice structuring arguments quickly
  • Read complex articles to improve passage comprehension
